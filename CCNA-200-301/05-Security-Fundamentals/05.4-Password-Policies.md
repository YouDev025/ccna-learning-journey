# Password Policies

## Definition
Password policies are organization-wide rules that define how passwords must be created, changed, and protected.

## Simple explanation
A password policy turns good password habits into enforceable standards.

## Common policy requirements
- Minimum password length
- Complexity rules such as uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
- Password history so old passwords cannot be reused immediately
- Expiration period for regular rotation
- Lockout thresholds after repeated failed attempts
- Password recovery procedures that verify identity carefully

## Why policies matter
- Reduce the chance of weak passwords
- Improve account security
- Lower the risk of brute-force attacks

## Example
A company policy may require passwords of at least 12 characters and prohibit reuse of the previous 5 passwords.

## Exam-style note
Password policies are most effective when combined with MFA, account monitoring, and secure password storage.

## Quick review
Password policies strengthen security by enforcing consistent password practices.
