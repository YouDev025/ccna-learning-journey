# Two-Tier Network Design

## Definition
A two-tier campus design uses an access layer and a collapsed core/distribution layer.

## Layers
| Layer | Main role |
|---|---|
| Access | Connects endpoints, APs, phones, and printers |
| Collapsed core/distribution | Aggregates access switches, routes VLANs, applies policy, connects to WAN/Internet |

## Benefits
- Simpler than a three-tier design.
- Lower equipment cost.
- Easier to manage in smaller environments.

## Limitations
- Less scalable than three-tier.
- Core/distribution devices can become critical failure points.
- Growth may require redesign.

## Example
Several access switches connect to two multilayer switches. Those switches provide inter-VLAN routing, redundancy, and uplinks to the firewall or WAN router.

## Exam focus
Two-tier is also called collapsed core because the core and distribution layers are combined.

## Quick review
Two-tier design fits smaller networks that need access switching and a combined aggregation/routing layer.
