# WAN

## Definition
A Wide Area Network, or WAN, connects networks across large geographic distances, such as branches, data centers, cloud environments, and headquarters.

## Common WAN connections
- Leased line
- MPLS
- Broadband internet
- Cellular
- Metro Ethernet
- VPN over internet
- SD-WAN overlay

## LAN vs WAN
| Feature | LAN | WAN |
|---|---|---|
| Scope | Building or campus | Cities, countries, global |
| Ownership | Usually organization-owned | Often provider-managed |
| Speed/latency | Usually faster/lower latency | Often slower/higher latency |

## Important WAN concepts
- Provider edge: service provider side of the connection.
- Customer edge: customer router or firewall side.
- Latency: delay across the link.
- Jitter: variation in delay.
- SLA: service-level agreement for provider service.

## Example
A branch router connects to headquarters through an encrypted VPN over broadband internet.

## Exam focus
WANs connect separate sites and often involve service providers, VPNs, or SD-WAN. Watch for latency, jitter, and SLA terms.

## Quick review
WANs extend connectivity beyond one local network or campus.
