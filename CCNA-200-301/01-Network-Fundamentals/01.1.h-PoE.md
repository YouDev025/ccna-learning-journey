# Power over Ethernet (PoE)

## Definition
Power over Ethernet, or PoE, provides electrical power and data over the same twisted-pair Ethernet cable.

## Simple explanation
A device such as an IP phone or wireless AP can get network connectivity and power from one switch port.

## Common PoE devices
- Wireless access points
- IP phones
- Security cameras
- Badge readers
- Small IoT devices

## Important terms
- PSE: Power Sourcing Equipment, usually a PoE switch or injector.
- PD: Powered Device, the device receiving power.
- PoE budget: total power a switch can provide across ports.
- PoE injector: device that adds power to an Ethernet cable when the switch does not support PoE.

## Common standards
| Standard | Common name | Approximate power at PSE |
|---|---|---|
| 802.3af | PoE | 15.4 W |
| 802.3at | PoE+ | 30 W |
| 802.3bt | PoE++ / UPoE | Higher power classes |

## Example
An AP connected to a PoE switch receives power and network access through a single Ethernet cable. If the switch PoE budget is exhausted, the AP may not power on.

## Troubleshooting checklist
- Does the switch support PoE?
- Is the port enabled for PoE?
- Is the switch power budget available?
- Does the powered device require more power than the port can provide?
- Is the cable good and within Ethernet distance limits?

## Exam focus
PoE is especially important for APs, IP phones, and cameras because these devices are often installed where power outlets are inconvenient.

## Quick review
PoE carries power and data on the same Ethernet cable.
