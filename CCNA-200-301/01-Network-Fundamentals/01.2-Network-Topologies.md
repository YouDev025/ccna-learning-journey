# Network Topologies

## Definition
A network topology describes how devices and links are arranged. It can describe physical cabling, logical traffic flow, or a design model.

## Common topology types
- Point-to-point: one direct connection between two devices.
- Star: devices connect to a central switch.
- Mesh: devices have multiple paths between them.
- Hub-and-spoke: branch sites connect to a central site.
- Two-tier: access and collapsed core/distribution.
- Three-tier: access, distribution, and core.
- Spine-leaf: data center design with predictable east-west traffic.

## Physical vs logical
| Type | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Physical topology | Actual cable and device layout | PCs cabled to an access switch |
| Logical topology | How traffic flows | VLANs, subnets, routing paths |

## Exam focus
Know redundancy, single points of failure, and the difference between physical and logical topology.

## Quick review
Topology explains network layout and helps predict how traffic and failures behave.
